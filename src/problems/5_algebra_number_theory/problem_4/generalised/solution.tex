From the angle triplication formula for the sine function, it follows that
$\mfrac{1}{2} = \sin\paren{\ang{30}} = - 4 \sin^3\paren{\ang{10}} + 3 \sin\paren{\ang{10}}$.
Hence, $\sin\paren{\ang{10}}$ is a root of the polynomial $8 x^3 - 6 x + 1$.
By the rational root theorem, the only possible rational roots of that polynomial are
$1, -1, \mfrac{1}{2}, - \mfrac{1}{2}, \mfrac{1}{4}, - \mfrac{1}{4}, \mfrac{1}{8}, - \mfrac{1}{8}$.
By inspection, none of those is a root of the polynomial.
(Alternatively, the polynomial is irreducible by Eisenstein's criterion
applied to the coefficients in reverse order, with the prime number $2$).
Hence $\sin\paren{\ang{10}}$ is irrational,
with minimal polynomial $x^3 - \mfrac{3}{4} x + \mfrac{1}{8}$,
and algebraic degree $3$.

From the power reduction formulas for the cosine function, it follows that
$\cos^2\paren{\ang{10}} = \mfrac{1}{2} \cos\paren{\ang{20}} + \mfrac{1}{2}$.
From the angle triplication formula for the cosine function, it follows that
$\mfrac{1}{2} = \cos\paren{\ang{60}} = 4 \cos^3\paren{\ang{20}} - 3 \cos\paren{\ang{20}}$.
Hence $\cos\paren{\ang{20}}$ is a root of the polynomial $8 x^3 - 6 x - 1$.
By the rational root theorem, the only possible rational roots of that polynomial are
$1, -1, \mfrac{1}{2}, - \mfrac{1}{2}, \mfrac{1}{4}, - \mfrac{1}{4}, \mfrac{1}{8}, - \mfrac{1}{8}$.
By inspection, none of those is a root of the polynomial.
(Alternatively, the polynomial is irreducible by Eisenstein's criterion
applied to the coefficients in reverse order, with the prime number $2$).
Hence $\cos\paren{\ang{20}}$ is irrational,
and so $\cos\paren{\ang{10}}$ is also irrational (since its square is irrational).

From the angle triplication formula for the tangent function, it follows that
$\mfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \tan\paren{\ang{30}}
  = \mfrac{\tan^3\paren{\ang{10}} - 3 \tan\paren{\ang{10}}}
          {3 \tan^2\paren{\ang{10}} - 1}$.
Hence, $\tan\paren{\ang{10}}$ is a root of the polynomial
$3 \paren{x^3 - 3 x}^2 - \paren{3 x^2 - 1}^2
 = 3 x^6 - 27 x^4 + 33 x^2 - 1$.
This polynomial is irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}\sqbk{x}$,
by Eisenstein's criterion applied to the coefficients in reverse order, with the prime number $3$.
In particular, it has no rational roots,
hence $\tan\paren{\ang{10}}$ is irrational,
with minimal polynomial $x^6 - 9 x^4 + 11 x^2 - \mfrac{1}{3}$,
and algebraic degree $6$.
