The following is an infinite sequence of solutions $\paren{a_n, b_n}$:
\begin{equation*}
\begin{cases}
  \paren{a_0,       b_0}       &= \, \paren{0, 1} \\
  \paren{a_{n + 1}, b_{n + 1}} &= \, \paren{3 a_{n} + 2 b_{n} + 1, \, 4 a_{n} + 3 b_{n} + 2}
\end{cases}
\end{equation*}
This can be seen by induction on $n$:
for $n = 0$ it is trivial;
then, assuming that the relation holds for $n$:
\begin{align*}
  a_{n + 1}^2 + \paren{a_{n + 1} + 1}^2
  &= 2 a_{n + 1}^2 + 2 a_{n + 1} + 1 = \\
  &= 2 \paren{3 a_{n} + 2 b_{n} + 1}^2 + 2 \paren{3 a_{n} + 2 b_{n} + 1} + 1 = \\
  &= 18 a_{n}^{2} + 8 b_{n}^{2} + 24 a_{n} b_{n} + 18 a_{n} + 12 b_{n} + 5 \\[1ex]
  %
  b_{n + 1}^2
  &= \paren{4 a_{n} + 3 b_{n} + 2}^2 = \\
  &= 16 a_{n}^{2} + 9 b_{n}^{2} + 24 a_{n} b_{n} + 16 a_{n} + 12 b_{n} + 4
\end{align*}
They are equal, since their difference is $b_{n}^2 - 2 a_{n}^2 - 2 a_{n} - 1$,
which is $0$, by the inductive hypothesis.
