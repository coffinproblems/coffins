The digit expansion of a number $a \in \ointerval{0}{1}$ has $0$ as first digit,
then for every $n \in \mathbb{N}$,
the digits $\paren{2^n + 1}$-th to $2^{n+1}$-th
are the opposite of the digits $1$-st to $2^{n}$-th, respectively,
where the opposite of the digit $1$ is the digit $0$, and viceversa.
Prove that $a$ is irrational.
