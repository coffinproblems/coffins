Let $R, S, T$ be sets, with $\card\paren{R} \geq 2$, $\card\paren{S} \geq 2$, and $\card\paren{T} \geq 3$.
Let $f : R \times S \to T$ be such that $\card\paren{\img\paren{f}} \geq 3$.
The elements $a \in R$ and $b \in S$ are such that the functions
$S \to T : y \mapsto f\paren{a, y}$ and
$R \to T : x \mapsto f\paren{x, b}$
are not constant.\newline
Prove that there exist $p, r \in R$ and $q, s \in S$ such that
$f\paren{p, q}$, $f\paren{r, q}$, $f\paren{p, s}$ are all distinct.
