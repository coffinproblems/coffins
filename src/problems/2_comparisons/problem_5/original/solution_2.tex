Let $r \in \ointerval{0}{1}$,
and consider the function $f : \mathbb{R}^{+} \to \mathbb{R}^{+} : x \mapsto x^r$.

$f$ is strictly concave (that is, $-f$ is strictly convex),
since for all $x \in \mathbb{R}^{+}$
$\, f^{\prime\prime}\paren{x} = r \paren{r - 1} x^{r - 2} < 0$,
because $r > 0$, $r - 1 < 0$, and $x > 0$. \newline
Hence, for any $x, y \in \mathbb{R}^{+}$ with $x \neq y$,
and for any $t \in \ointerval{0}{1}$:
$\, f\paren{t x + \paren{1 - t} y} > t f\paren{x} + \paren{1 - t} f\paren{y}$.

Putting $r = \dfrac{1}{3}$, $x = 3 + \sqrt[3]{3}$, $y = 3 - \sqrt[3]{3}$, $t = \dfrac{1}{2}$,
the claimed inequality is obtained.
