Let the polynomial $p\paren{x, y, z} = \paren{x - y}^2 + \paren{y - z}^2 + \paren{z - x}^2$,
and recall the polynomial identity:
\begin{equation*}
  x^3 + y^3 + z^3 - 3 x y z = \paren{x + y + z} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \mspace{2mu} p\paren{x, y, z}
\end{equation*}
Note that the polynomial $p$ evaluated at real values is always non-negative, since it is a sum of squares.
Substituting $x = \sqrt[3]{413}$, $y = - 6$, $z = - \sqrt[3]{3}$ in the above identity gives:
\begin{align*}
  \frac{1}{2} \mspace{2mu} \paren{\mspace{-2mu} \sqrt[3]{413} - \paren*[\big]{6 \mspace{2mu} + \mspace{-1mu} \sqrt[3]{3} \mspace{2mu}} \mspace{-2mu}}
  \mspace{-2mu} \cdot \mspace{-1.2mu} p\paren*[\big]{\mspace{-2mu} \sqrt[3]{413} \mspace{1mu}, \mspace{1mu} - 6, \mspace{1mu} - \sqrt[3]{3} \mspace{2mu}}
  &= 413 - 216 - 3 - 3 \cdot 6 \cdot \mspace{-1.5mu} \sqrt[3]{3 \cdot 413} = \\
  &= 194 - 18 \sqrt[3]{1239} = \\
  &= 2 \cdot \mspace{-2mu} \paren*[\big]{97 - 9 \sqrt[3]{1239} \mspace{2mu}}
\end{align*}
Hence, if $97 > 9 \sqrt[3]{1239}$, then $\sqrt[3]{413}$ is greater than $6 \mspace{2mu} + \mspace{-1mu} \sqrt[3]{3}$,
and if $97 < 9 \sqrt[3]{1239}$, then $\sqrt[3]{413} < 6 \mspace{2mu} + \mspace{-1mu} \sqrt[3]{3}$.
Now:
\begin{align*}
  \paren{\frac{97}{9}}^3
  &= \paren{10 + \frac{7}{9}}^3
   > 1000 + 3 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{7}{9} + 3 \cdot 10 \cdot \frac{49}{81} = \\
  &= 1000 + \frac{700}{3} + \frac{490}{27}
   > 1000 + 233 + 10 > \\
  &> 1239
\end{align*}
It follows that $97 > 9 \sqrt[3]{1239 \mspace{1mu}} \mspace{1mu}$.
So, in conclusion:
\begin{equation*}
  6 \mspace{2mu} + \mspace{-1mu} \sqrt[3]{3 \mspace{1mu}} \mspace{2mu}
  <
  \sqrt[3]{413 \mspace{1mu}}
\end{equation*}
