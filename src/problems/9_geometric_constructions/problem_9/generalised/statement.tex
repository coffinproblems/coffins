Determine for which $n, k \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}$ it is possible,
given $k$ segments of lengths $a_1, \dotsc, a_k$ on the plane,
to construct, using only straightedge and compass,
a segment of length $b$ such that:
\begin{equation*}
 \sqrt[n]{b \vphantom{a_i}} = \sum\limits_{i = 1}^{k} {\sqrt[n]{a_i \vphantom{b}}}
\end{equation*}
Note: a segment of length $1$ is not given.
