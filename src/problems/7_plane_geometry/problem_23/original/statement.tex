Let $ABC$ be a triangle.
Let $M$ be the midpoint of $\overline{AC}$.
Let $\overline{CL}$ be the angle bisector of $\widehat{BCA}$,
with $L \in \overline{AB}$.
Let $P$ be the intersection point of $\overline{CL}$ and $\overline{BM}$.
Prove that
$\mfrac{\overline{CP}}{\overline{PL}}
 - \mfrac{\overline{AC}}{\overline{CB}}
 = 1$.
