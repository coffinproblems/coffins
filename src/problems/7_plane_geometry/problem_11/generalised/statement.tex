For any partition $U$ of $\mathbb{R}^2$, let
$D_U = \setbuild{a \in \mathbb{R}^{+}}
         {\exists \, T \in U
          \text{ such that } \exists \, p, q \in T
          \text{ with } \dist{p}{q} = a}$.
What is the largest cardinal $\alpha$
such that for every partition $U$ of $\mathbb{R}^{2}$
with $\card\paren{U} = \alpha$,
the set $D_U$ is the whole $\mathbb{R}^{+}$?

(What about partitions of $\mathbb{R}^m$?)
