Let $a, b, c$ be the side lengths of a triangle,
and let $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ be the measures of their opposite angles, respectively.
Prove that:
\begin{equation*}
  \frac{b + c - 2a}{\sin\paren{\mfrac{\alpha}{2}}}
  + \frac{c + a - 2b}{\sin\paren{\mfrac{\beta}{2}}}
  + \frac{a + b - 2c}{\sin\paren{\mfrac{\gamma}{2}}}
  \geq 0
\end{equation*}
