Let $ABC$ be a triangle.
Let $\gamma$ be its circumcircle.
Let $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3$ be circles such that
$\alpha_1$ is tangent to $\overline{BC}$, to $\overline{CA}$, and to $\gamma$ (internally);
$\alpha_2$ is tangent to $\overline{AB}$, to $\overline{CA}$, and to $\gamma$ (internally);
$\alpha_3$ is tangent to $\overline{AB}$, to $\overline{BC}$, and to $\gamma$ (internally).
Determine the radius of $\gamma$, given the radii of $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3$.

Alernative formulation.

Three circles are each tangent to a (distinct) unordered pair of (distinct) sides of a triangle
and to the circumcircle of the triangle (internally).
Determine the radius of the circumcircle, given the radii of the three circles.
