The complex number $\cos\paren{\ang{1}} + i \sin\paren{\ang{1}}$ is:
\begin{align*}
 \cos\paren{\ang{1}} + i \sin\paren{\ang{1}}
 &= e^{i \paren{\frac{\pi}{18} - \frac{\pi}{20}}}
  = e^{i \frac{\pi}{18}} \cdot
    \big( \! \cos\paren{\ang{9}} - i \sin\paren{\ang{9}} \big) = \\
 &= \frac{1}{4} \, \sqrt[3]{\frac{\sqrt{3} + i}{2}}
    \, \cdot
    \Bigg( \,
      \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2} \, \big(\sqrt{5} + 1 \big)
      + \sqrt{5 - \sqrt{5}}
      \; - i \, \bigg(
      \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2} \, \big(\sqrt{5} + 1 \big)
      - \sqrt{5 - \sqrt{5}} \,
      \bigg) \!
   \Bigg)
\end{align*}
Then, $\sin\paren{\ang{1}}$ and $\cos\paren{\ang{1}}$
are its imaginary part and its real part, respectively.

The complex number $\cos\paren{\ang{2}} + i \sin\paren{\ang{2}}$ is:
\begin{align*}
 \cos\paren{\ang{2}} + i \sin\paren{\ang{2}}
 &= e^{i \, \paren{\frac{\pi}{9} - \frac{\pi}{10}}}
  = e^{i \frac{\pi}{9}} \cdot
    \big( \! \cos\paren{\ang{18}} - i \sin\paren{\ang{18}} \big) = \\
 &= \frac{1}{4} \, \sqrt[3]{\frac{1 + i \sqrt{3}}{2}}
    \, \cdot
    \Bigg( \!
      \sqrt{2} \, \sqrt{5 + \sqrt{5}}
      \, - \, i \, \frac{\sqrt{5} - 1}{2}
   \Bigg)
\end{align*}
Then, $\sin\paren{\ang{2}}$ and $\cos\paren{\ang{2}}$
are its imaginary part and its real part, respectively.
