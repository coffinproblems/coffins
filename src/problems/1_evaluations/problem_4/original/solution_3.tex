The number of digits of a positive integer $n$ in base $b$ is
$\floor{\log_{b}\paren{n} + 1}$.
Thus, the required number of digits is $\floor{100 \log_{10}\paren{125} + 1}$,
(and the problem is essentially that of evaluating
$\log_{10}\paren{125}$ to two decimal places).

First notice that $\log_{10}\paren{2} > \mfrac{3}{10}$,
since $2^{10} = 1024 > 1000 = 10^3$.
Then, observe that
$3 = \log_{10}\paren{1000}
   = \log_{10}\paren{2^3 \cdot 5^3}
   = \log_{10}\paren{125} + 3 \log_{10}\paren{2}$.
From this, the following upper bound is deduced:
$100 \log_{10}\paren{125}
 = 300 - 300 \log_{10}\paren{2}
 < 300 - 300 \cdot \mfrac{3}{10}
 = 210$.

Now observe that
$\log_{10}\paren{125}
 = \log_{10}\paren{100 \cdot \mfrac{5}{4}}
 = 2 + \log_{10}\paren{\mfrac{5}{4}}$.
The following steps show that $100 \log_{10}\paren{\mfrac{5}{4}} > 9$:
\begin{align*}
 \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{4}   &= \paren{1 + \frac{1}{4}}^{4} = \paren{1 + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{16} }^2
   > 1 + 1 + \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{8} = 2 + \frac{3}{8} \\
 \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{8}   &> \paren{2 + \frac{3}{8}}^2
   > 4 + \frac{3}{2} = 5 + \frac{1}{2} \\
 \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{16}  &> \paren{5 + \frac{1}{2}}^2 > 25 + 5 = 30 \\
 \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{33}  &> \frac{5}{4} \cdot 30^2 > 1000 \\
 \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{100} &> \paren{\frac{5}{4}}^{99} > 1000^3 = 10^9
\end{align*}
In conclusion:
$100 \log_{10}\paren{125}
 = 200 + 100 \log_{10}\paren{\mfrac{5}{4}}
 > 200 + 9 = 209$.\newline
Therefore, $209 < \log_{10}\paren{125^{100}} < 210$.
It follows that $\floor{\log_{10}\paren{125^{100}} + 1} = 210$.
