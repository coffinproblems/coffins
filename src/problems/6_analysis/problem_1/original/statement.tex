Let $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ with $a < b$.
Let $f : \cinterval{a}{b} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a non-decreasing function.
Determine which points $c \in \cinterval{a}{b}$ minimize the value:
\ifluatex
  \begin{equation*}
    \int\limits_{a}^{c} {\mspace{-9mu} f\paren{x} - f\paren{a} \dd x} \, +
    \int\limits_{c}^{b} {\mspace{-9mu} f\paren{b} - f\paren{x} \dd x}
  \end{equation*}
\else
  \begin{equation*}
    \int\limits_{a}^{c} {\mspace{-3mu} f\paren{x} - f\paren{a} \dd x} \, +
    \int\limits_{c}^{b} {\mspace{-3mu} f\paren{b} - f\paren{x} \dd x}
  \end{equation*}
\fi
